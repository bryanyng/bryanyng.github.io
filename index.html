Experience

Internship at Lalamove HK

Nov 2016 - Feb 2017

Lalamove is an on-demand logistics company originating from Hong Kong. They currently have both a mobile and web app, which serve as gateways to utilizing their services. During my internship at their headquarters office in Hong Kong, I have gained many valuable experiences. Not only did it allow me to cultivate my programming skills, but also my interpersonal skills.

The internship consisted of a main goal. Which is to develop the ‘Proof of Delivery’ feature for the existing app. The idea is that the user who called for their services are able to confirm that their package has been delivered, via receiving their signature on the app. Despite lacking experience in mobile and web development, I was very fortunate to have professional support from the other teams present at the office. Over the course of the internship we were encouraged to do a lot of the learning ourselves with the materials provided, and seeking the relevant team members for help when necessary. Which allowed me to develop good interpersonal skills and build tight connections in the office.

To reach this goal, we followed the scrum process by conducting daily stand-ups, sprint planning, and sprint reviews. Jira (from Atlassian) was introduced to us as the tool to use to manage our project, where the project was broken down into user stories for us to complete. It was also necessary for us to follow the company’s practices in version control. For example, it was required of us to follow their guidelines on writing commit messages in Git. The guidelines provided a way of identifying what kind of update was made on a particular repository at a glance. Such as “FIX: …” meaning a fix was made, and “UPT: …” meaning an update. This gave me realistic experience in project management with Jira and Git where we actively collaborated on the project between us as interns.

Unfortunately, I am unable to share any of my work done at Lalamove. But I would like to once again thank them for allowing an opportunity for me to gain valuable experience, and broadening my technological knowledge during my time there.

Android App - Recipedia

For this project, we managed to implement an app made for people who want to share and browse recipes for food. The final product was able make corresponding api calls to Google's Firebase, where we stored user information and recipe data. As seen in the screenshots below, users are able to register and login to their own accounts. And even if they are not logged in, users may still search and browse for recipes of their liking. While logged in, users are able to save recipes that they like and upload to share recipes of their own.
